April 4, 2019

Bassmaster Elite at Lake Hartwell: April 4th, 2019. Professional Fisherman Visit Anderson,SC

Anderson, SC at Lake Hartwell.

Just off of I-85, at the South Carolina-Georgia border

56,000-acre

Fishing in this area is renowned by anglers across the state.

The lake is inhabited by striped and hybrid bass, largemouth, crappie, bream and catfish

Weather - Conditions

Sunny - No Wind

Air Temps - 50’s Water Temp - 55+

Topwater Action for schooling fish.

Fish are being caught on topwater walking plugs for schooling spotted bass eating blueback herring that is found in the lake. Fish are spitting up the blueback herring as the anglers are bringing them to the surface.

Pro’s are turning to the Ned Rig when the schooling activity diminishes.

Other Anglers: Chasing Spawners

Light winds are allowing the anglers to look at the fish in the clear water of Hartwell.

A few anglers have beds marked in the back of coves and are sight fishing for them. Pro Angler Chris Johnston, Peterborough Ontario has a big fish locked on the bed that he can see.

Spray Bottle in Hand

Clark Wendlandt fishing for spawning fish and let’s us in on a good tip about pollen being too thick on top the water to see the spawning bass.

If there is pollen on the water you can use a spray bottle with dish soap in it to clear the water and see down below.

Clark Wendlandt

A few pro’s have taken up smaller swimbaits and are casting them around as the wind is picking up slightly.

Tough Bite so far with smaller fish being caught in general today. That should change as the tournament progresses over the weekend.
